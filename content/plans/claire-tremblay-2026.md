**Post-retirement tax optimization — the core of this plan:**

Claire's RRSP ($485k, growing to ~$540k by 60) will eventually be forced into RRIF with mandatory minimum withdrawals starting at 72. If she waits until then, the mandatory withdrawals + CPP + OAS could push her marginal rate back to 41%+ and trigger OAS clawback (threshold ~$90k).

The strategy is to **smooth withdrawals across the low-income years (60–72)** while her marginal rate is much lower.

| Age | Taxable Income | Marginal Rate (QC) | Source |
|-----|---------------:|-------------------:|--------|
| 57–59 (working) | $86,000 | ~41% | Salary |
| 60–61 | $39,200 | ~29% | RRIF $28,800 + non-reg $10,400 |
| 62–66 | $49,400 | ~33% | RRIF $28,800 + non-reg $10,400 + CPP $10,200 |
| 67–71 | $57,800 | ~37% | RRIF $28,800 + non-reg $10,400 + CPP $10,200 + OAS $8,400 |
| 72+ (mandatory RRIF) | $65,000+ | ~38%+ | Depends on balance remaining |

**Key insight:** By drawing down $28,800/yr from RRIF starting at 60, Claire's marginal rate averages ~33% across 12 years instead of spiking to 41%+ after 72. Estimated lifetime tax savings: $35,000–$50,000. *(Links to Goal #3)*

**TFSA withdrawals are tax-free** and should be used to top up spending in years where RRIF + other income falls short of $48k, without affecting the marginal rate.

**Strategy:**

Taking at 62 fills the $4,000 income gap from years 60–62 and provides steady income without increasing RRIF withdrawals. Taking at 65 gives more per month but leaves a longer gap to bridge.
- **OAS at 67**: Defer 2 years from 65 for a 12% increase. Claire's income at 65 (~$49k) is well below the clawback threshold (~$90k), so there's no clawback risk, but the 12% increase is a free annuity boost. Bridging the 2 extra years is easy with existing assets.
